Geography of Catbalogan City
Catbalogan City is located along the western coast of Samar Island, facing the Samar Sea. It has a total land area of 274.22 square kilometers (105.86 sq mi). The city is bounded by the municipalities of Tarangnan and Gandara to the north, Jiabong to the south, and Wright (Paranas) to the east. To the west, it is bordered by the Maqueda Bay, which provides access to fishing and maritime activities.

The city is characterized by rolling hills, coastal plains, and mangrove forests. The inland areas consist of mountains and rivers, contributing to its rich biodiversity. The Pan-Philippine Highway (AH26) runs through Catbalogan, making it a key transportation hub in Samar.

The city's coastal location makes it vulnerable to typhoons and heavy rainfall, especially during the rainy season from June to November. However, it also benefits from a tropical rainforest climate, supporting agriculture and fisheries, which are major economic activities in the area.
